External surface transfer are crucial for the overall hygrothermal performance as the refer to the rate of heat and mass transfer from the environment to the material surface or vice versa. The mass transfer will define the wetting and drying of the building materials. The different mechanism include:

  • Convective heat transfer.
  • Radiative heat transfer (e.g. sun radiation or longwave from the sky).
  • Vapor mass transfer.
  • Liquid mass transfer.

The way these coefficients are spread on the outer surface defines the temperature and moisture distribution over the building shell. Uneven distribution may lead to specific areas being more susceptible to moisture buildup, some parts cooler, or having better drying potential. For instance, in a tall building, the stack effect and wind pressure differences can result in varied coefficient distribution, affecting moisture and temperature across different levels.
