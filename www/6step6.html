Here, the indoor climate parameters are determined. These are crucial for various factors such as energy consumption, indoor air quality, and occupant comfort. The parameters also have an impact on the wetting and drying potential of materials from the inside of the building. Indoor climate, including temperature, relative humidity, and ventilation conditions. It's important to represent conditions that match the building's anticipated use, which might require slightly elevated humidity levels for a conservative approach.

Superior

The indoor climate is defined by measured values for the specific building, or a building in the same outdoor climate and similar user behavior, or defined by the setpoint-values of the HVAC systems.
The indoor climate is defined by measured values for the specific building, or a building in the same outdoor climate and similar user behavior, or defined by the setpoint-values of the HVAC systems. Note: this approach does not account for changes in indoor climate over time.

Advanced

Numerical building simulation software is used to calculate the indoor climate during or prior to the HAM simulation. The main emphasis is on precise modeling of both indoor moisture load and ventilation conditions to ensure an accurate representation of reality.

Minimum

The indoor climate is determined by the national standard or based on an adaptive indoor climate. In case of a new design assessment, the indoor conditions are set to accommodate the highest possible moisture loads, to represent the most critical use scenario.
The indoor climate is determined by the national standard or based on an adaptive indoor climate. According to EN ISO 15026 Annex D, an adaptive internal humidity can be computed for heated buildings based on the outdoor temperature. In case of a new design assessment, the indoor conditions are set to accommodate the highest possible moisture loads, to represent the most critical use scenario.
