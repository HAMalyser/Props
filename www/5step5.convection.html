The convective heat transfer coefficient, hce, represents the rate at which heat is transferred through convective heat transfer, which is the transfer of heat through the movement of the air boundary layer. Wind speed and direction will have a crucial role in this transfer as well as the roughness of the building surface and the temperature differences. Going up to advanced and even superior means that the dependency parameters are defined more precisly.

Superior

Heat transfer is simulated using Computational Fluid Dynamics for different wind speeds and orientations, with enough refinement at the boundary layer of the wall.

Advanced

Use distinct expressions for convective heat transfer coefficients. These should consider the effect of wind speed, building height, building width and wind direction, specifically at the windward facades.
Use distinct expressions for convective heat transfer coefficients. These should consider the effect of wind speed, building height, building width and wind direction, specifically at the windward facades. An example for such approach is developped by Montazeri and Blocken (2018).

Minimum

If no wind-dependent transfer coefficient is available, a constant value from literature can be used. If local speed is known, calculate the surface heat transfer coefficent according to EN15026.
If no wind-dependent transfer coefficient is available, a constant value from literature can be used. If local speed is known, calculate the surface heat transfer coefficent according to EN15026. hce = 4 + 4*v with: v = wind speed [m/s]
