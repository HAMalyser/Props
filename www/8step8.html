This section focusses on the field and surface conditions of cells in the model. Cell conditions can relate either to the entire cell or just to its surface. The former is referred to as field condition, whereas the latter is known as surface condition and includes resistances and flux-resistance properties. Field conditions can simulate various source and sink phenomena, such as the movement or leakage of moisture, air, and heat. Surface or resistance conditions, on the other hand, introduce barriers to air, moisture, and heat transfers. The surface conditions can also be used to model a simplified interface resistance between two materials to avoid assuming a continuous pore system at the interface.
